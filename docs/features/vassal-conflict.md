# Vassal System & Conflict

**Status:** Draft

## Vassal System

When a city is militarily overwhelmed, the attacker chooses: **raze or vassalize**.

| Choice | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Raze | City is gone — attacker gains nothing ongoing |
| Vassalize | Vassal keeps their city and continues playing; attacker receives tribute (% of trade output) and prestige from dominion |

**Vassal escape options:**
- The vassal can be liberated by allied forces
- The vassal can buy independence with accumulated gold

**Attacker constraint:**
- Over-vassalizing drains Authority rapidly
- Each vassal adds upkeep; conquest is a calculated economic decision, not a pure power play

## Tile Influence and Borders

Tiles far from a city center slowly lose loyalty unless maintained through roads, outposts, or sufficient prestige. A player can passively influence a neighbor's outer tiles by building closer to them.

This creates natural border friction without requiring purely military resolution. Prestige and physical presence matter at the edges of claimed territory.
