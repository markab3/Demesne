# Labor — Retainers

Retainers are salaried direct employees who can be given explicit orders. They wait for orders and do nothing until assigned — unlike specialists who operate autonomously.

## Retainer Types

| Type | Salary | Primary Function |
|---|---|---|
| Professional Soldier | Gold per tick | Standing garrison, offensive campaigns |
| General | Gold + Authority upkeep | Commands armies outside territory |
| Knight | Gold + equipment upkeep | Elite military unit, individual record |
| Spy | Gold | Espionage — intercept, poach, sabotage, scout |
| Diplomat | Gold | Carries messages, negotiates treaties |
| Herald | Gold | Manages reputation, formal declarations, extends messenger range |

## Salary

Salary is deducted from the city treasury every tick.

**Missed payment cascade:**
1. One missed payment: morale warning
2. Two missed payments: effectiveness penalty, some desert
3. Sustained non-payment: mass desertion; generals and knights may defect with forces intact

## Consumption Profile

| Retainer | Food | Clothing | Equipment | Luxury |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Professional Soldier | Salted meat or bread | Basic clothing | Weapons and armor wear | Beer or spirits |
| Knight | Fine food | Fine clothing | High-quality weapons and armor wear | Wine, luxury goods |
| General | Fine food | Fine clothing | — | Wine, prestige goods |
| Spy | Standard food | Standard clothing | — | — |
| Diplomat | Fine food | Fine clothing | — | Wine |
| Herald | Standard food | Standard clothing | — | — |

Knights and Generals consuming luxury goods is not optional — it is part of their upkeep. Equipment degrades over ticks and must be replenished from the stockpile to maintain effectiveness.

## Retainer Idle Cost

Retainers wait for orders. A spy sits idle until given a mission. This makes them expensive to stockpile without purpose — the salary runs whether or not they are doing anything.
