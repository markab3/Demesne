# Quality System

**Status:** Draft

## Craft Rating

Each industry has an independent **Craft Rating** (continuous 1–100 scale) that determines the quality of finished goods produced.

- Climbs slowly with sustained specialist employment, relevant guild buildings, and consistent output
- Decays gradually if specialists are dismissed or buildings fall into disrepair
- Same inputs with a higher Craft Rating produce higher quality output
- Quality propagates up production chains — every step in the chain contributes

## Specialization Incentive

Specialist pools are finite. Investing in one industry's Craft Rating means another plateaus. Cities that attempt to do everything produce mediocre quality across the board. Cities that commit to a specialism become the only viable source of high-quality versions of that good — which is the foundation of the trade system.

The wool chain illustrates this: Raw Wool → Washed Wool → Yarn → Wool Cloth → Dyed Cloth → Fine Clothing is six steps. No single city should find it efficient to run all six at high quality. The historical pattern — raw wool from pastoral cities, finishing in specialist ones — should emerge naturally.

## Specialist Tiers

Within each trade:

| Tier | Craft Rating Contribution | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Apprentice | Low | Cheap to employ |
| Journeyman | Moderate | Moderate cost |
| Master | High | Expensive, scarce |

Masters are rare enough that losing one to a rival city is a meaningful strategic event.
