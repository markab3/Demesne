# Guild System

**Status:** Draft

The guild mediates between individual craftsmen and the lord. The player interacts with the **guild as a collective** for each trade rather than managing specialists individually.

## Guild Functions

- Negotiates commission rates and tax terms on behalf of all members in that trade
- Guild standing determines how much commission capacity is available and at what price
- A guild in good standing self-regulates — apprentices trained, quality maintained, disputes resolved internally
- A guild in poor standing reduces output and quality across all members in that trade

## The Guild Hall

The guild hall is one of the most important civic investments.

- Without it, specialists operate individually with no collective structure
- Individual specialists without guild structure are harder to direct and easier to lose one by one
- With a guild hall: accelerates Craft Rating and trains apprentices in that trade

## What the City Provides vs. What Specialists Provide

**What the city provides:**
- Market access and legal trading rights
- Physical security — walls, garrison, law enforcement
- Infrastructure — roads, wells, the market building
- Guild recognition — charters that allow specialists to take apprentices

**What specialists provide:**
- Periodic goods tax — a portion of output requisitioned
- Commission availability — directed production when required
- Guild fee — gold payment for their charter, renewed periodically
