# Resources

## Crops

Each crop has preferred temperature (T), precipitation (P), and altitude (Alt) ranges. Planting outside preferred conditions reduces yield.

| Crop | T | P | Alt | Output | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wheat | Temperate | Moderate | Low-Mid | Food staple | Broad tolerance workhorse grain |
| Rye | Cold | Moderate | Mid | Food staple | Survives poor soil, northern bias |
| Rice | Warm/Hot | Wet | Low | Food staple | River delta and flood plain only |
| Maize | Warm | Moderate | Low-Mid | Food staple | High yield, narrow temperature band |
| Barley | Cold-Temp | Arid | Mid | Food + brewing | Brewing output feeds luxury chain |
| Oats | Cold | Moderate | Mid | Food + fodder | Horse and livestock upkeep reduction |
| Root Vegetables | Cold-Temp | Moderate | Mid | Food staple | Famine buffer crop |
| Olives | Warm | Arid | Low-Mid | Luxury food + oil | Mediterranean profile, slow growth |
| Grapes | Temperate | Moderate | Mid | Luxury (wine) | High prestige, climate-sensitive |
| Hops | Temperate | Wet | Low-Mid | Luxury (beer) | Pairs with barley in brewing chain |
| Sugarcane | Hot | Wet | Low | Luxury | High prestige value, rare climate requirement |
| Cotton | Warm/Hot | Moderate | Low | Industrial | Textile chain input |
| Flax | Temperate | Moderate | Low-Mid | Industrial | Linen, rope, shipbuilding adjacency |
| Tobacco | Warm | Moderate | Low | Luxury | Pure prestige good, no food value |
| Spices | Hot | Wet | Low | Luxury | Extreme prestige, one or two deposits per map region |
| Woad | Temperate | Moderate | Low-Mid | Industrial | Blue dye source |
| Madder | Temperate | Moderate | Low-Mid | Industrial | Red dye source |
| Herbs | Temperate | Moderate | Mid | Medical/luxury | Apothecary chain, happiness bonus |
| Hemp | Temperate | Moderate | Low-Mid | Industrial | Rope, cordage |
| Timber | Cold-Temp | Wet | Mid-High | Industrial | Forest tiles only, not arable |

## Minerals

Mineral deposits vary in quantity per tile. Rock type is not modeled.

### Common — Structural

| Mineral | Terrain Bias | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|
| Iron Ore | Mid-high altitude | Weapons, tools, construction |
| Copper Ore | Mid altitude | Bronze, coinage |
| Tin | Low-mid altitude | Bronze alloy — requires copper pairing |
| Limestone | Low altitude | Masonry, mortar |
| Clay | Low, riverine | Ceramics, brick |
| Coal | Mid altitude | Fuel — multiplies smelting output |
| Salt | Coastal / arid flats | Food preservation, trade commodity |
| Sand / Silica | Coastal / desert | Glass production chain |

### Uncommon — Refined

| Mineral | Terrain Bias | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|
| Lead | Mid altitude | Pipes, weights, alchemy |
| Zinc | Mid altitude | Brass alloy with copper |
| Sulfur | Volcanic / geothermal | Alchemy, gunpowder if tech permits |
| Marble | High altitude | Prestige construction, sculpture |
| Gemstones | High altitude | Jewelry, luxury prestige chain |

### Rare — High Value

| Mineral | Terrain Bias | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|
| Silver | Mid-high altitude | Coinage, luxury goods, trade standard |
| Gold | Riverine placer / high altitude | Coinage, ultimate luxury good |
| Mithril | Deep mountain / magical strata | Exceptional weapons, armor, high prestige |
| Adamantine | Volcanic / deep terrain | Siege equipment, elite fortification |

Mithril and Adamantine deposits are rare enough to be geopolitically significant — small, expensive to extract, and naturally contested.
