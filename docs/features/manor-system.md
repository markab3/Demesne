# Manor System

Each inhabited tile is administered through a **manor** held by a noble house. The lord does not manage peasants directly — nobles do.

## Noble Function

- The noble takes a cut of the tile's peasant output before the city sees its tax share
- In exchange, the noble handles local administration and provides a fixed military obligation (feudal levy) each season
- Nobles with high loyalty pass through more of the goods tax and deliver their military obligation reliably
- Nobles with low loyalty skim more, deliver less, and may defect — taking the tile's peasant population with them or opening the tile to rival influence

## Noble Loyalty

| Factor | Effect |
|---|---|
| Gifts, titles, prestigious appointments | Positive |
| Granting additional tiles | Positive |
| Being ignored or over-extracted from | Negative |
| On the losing side of a war | Negative |

## Countering Noble Skimming

An **accountant specialist** reduces the amount nobles can skim regardless of their loyalty level, by improving administrative oversight. This is the primary tool for wresting back tax yield without raising noble loyalty directly.

A **steward** assigned to a specific manor reduces noble loyalty drain and improves local tax yield at that tile.
